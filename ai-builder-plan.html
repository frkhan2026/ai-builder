What the Time Audit Revealed

3 things that matter most. Full Time Audit →

1. The real work window is 4 hours a day — maximum. Daily standup at 8am, a hard stop at 3pm, and 12–14 hrs/week of recurring Akura meetings leave roughly 10am–12pm and 2–3pm as true discretionary time. On Thursdays (Sr. Staff + Faisal/Kavi + Cerevia back-to-back), that window collapses to zero before noon. Every automation should protect or extend this window, not add to the load inside it.
2. There is no post-conference follow-up system — and SCAI is happening right now. 10+ physicians are being engaged across SCAI (Apr 23–25). When the conference ends, follow-up defaults to memory, fragmented Slack threads, and whatever gets prioritized in a packed post-travel week. The 2-week post-conference window is when physician interest either converts to a sales conversation or stalls. Without a system to hand off to the sales team, momentum is lost.
3. Every NavIQ document spends 2 weeks in regulatory review across 3 systems. The NavIQ Sales Presentation required 3 Enlil DCO cycles plus SharePoint comment rounds with Theresa and Rob — all before SCAI. This pattern repeats for every conference deliverable. Faisal manually submits, tracks, and chases completion. The process is invisible until a deadline is missed.

What I'm Building

Sorted by impact: hours reclaimed × alignment with supporting the NavIQ commercial goal. Each card = one automation opportunity.

01

Every SCAI physician gets handed off to the sales team with context — before the post-conference window closes

Quick Win ~3 hrs/week reclaimed post-conference ⬆ Keeps sales team unblocked after every conference
Not started
Outcome Within 48 hours of each SCAI meeting, the sales team receives a structured handoff: physician name, institution, conversation summary, suggested next step, and an Asana task with deadline. Faisal triggers this with a 30-second Slack note from the conference floor — the agent handles the rest. Today SCAI is running Apr 23–25. After the conference, handoff defaults to memory, verbal debriefs, and fragmented Slack threads. The sales team starts the follow-up window without consistent context on what was discussed or what the next step should be for each physician. Automated During SCAI: Faisal sends a brief Slack note after each physician meeting ("Rosenfield — NavIQ pilot interest, wants data on outcomes, follow up in 2 weeks"). Agent drafts a personalized email for sales team review, creates an Asana task assigned to the right rep with a deadline, and logs the contact. Post-conference Monday digest shows all SCAI contacts with handoff status.
02

Daily brief runs in the cloud — no laptop wake-up required

Quick Win ~1 hr/week overhead eliminated ⬆ NavIQ priorities arrive before the 8am standup, reliably
Not started
Outcome At 6:50am PT, Faisal receives a NavIQ-first morning brief via email — regardless of whether his laptop is on. Sections: (1) Sales team handoffs needing attention, (2) Outstanding reg DCOs + deadlines, (3) Days to EuroPCR + open prep items, (4) Post-SCAI physician pipeline, (5) Japan distributor thread status. One email. Decision-ready before standup. Today A daily brief runs via Claude Cowork on a scheduled task — but it requires the personal laptop to be awake at home each morning to fire. When the laptop is closed or asleep, the brief doesn't run. The brief also surfaces general top-of-mind items rather than a NavIQ-first view. Automated Move the scheduled task to a cloud-hosted trigger (n8n, a server-side cron, or a remote Claude scheduled task) so it fires at 6:50am PT independent of any local machine. Expand the brief template with the 5 NavIQ-specific sections above. Delivered to Gmail, read on any device.

Not started
Outcome When web content is ready for regulatory review, the agent submits it, tracks approval across Enlil and SharePoint, posts status updates to Slack, nudges reviewers before deadlines slip, and confirms clearance before publish. The web team gets a green light automatically — Faisal doesn't manage the cycle. Today A new page for akuramedical.com is in progress. All external-facing NavIQ content requires regulatory approval before going live. Based on the NavIQ Sales Presentation (MKT-02601 Rev B) as the benchmark: 3 Enlil DCO cycles (04745 → 04814 → 04819) plus SharePoint comment rounds with Theresa and Rob — 2 weeks of manual tracking across 3 platforms to approve a single document. Website content will follow the same pattern. Automated "Ready for reg" in Slack (or a Google Drive status tag) triggers the agent: sends a formatted review request to Theresa and Rob with content link, compliance checklist, and publish deadline; monitors Enlil DCO status emails; posts progress to #reg-review; sends a 24-hr nudge if approval is stalled; confirms clearance and notifies the web team to proceed. Same workflow as the document review automation — one system, two use cases.
